About

Dr Ashraf obtained BSc in Aerospace Engineering from Sharif university of Technology in 2000, MSc in Reservoir Engineering from the same university in 2003 and PhD in Applied Mathematics in 2014 from University of Bergen in partnership with SINTEF ICT, Stuttgart University and Princeton University funded by MATMORA project to study Mathematical Modelling and Risk Assessment of Geological Storage of CO2. His doctoral thesis title was “Geological Storage of CO2: Sensitivity and Risk Analysis”. His doctoral programme involved a range of research activities from extensive simulation of flow in porous media, data driven modeling by polynomial approximation (arbitrary polynomial chaos expansion), variance based sesitivity analysis using Sobol indices, and Monte-Carlo Risk assement process . His MSc thesis was on “Water flooding model based on Stones method”. The outcome of the work was implemented to on an offshore field history. His BSc thesis was on “Experimental Study of Fuel Injection and Combustion”. The study investigated the effect of injector angle and injection pulse in the automobile engine on the thermodynamical combustion efficiency.
